Privacy Policy for Walkie Talkie Bluetooth by GIzanTech

Effective Date: August 30, 2025

GIzanTech ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Walkie Talkie Bluetooth mobile application ("App") on Android devices. Please read this policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms, please do not use the App.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following information:

  • Personal Information: When you use the App, we may collect personal information you voluntarily provide, such as your username, profile picture, or contact details, if you choose to share them for social media-like features (e.g., chat, voice messages, or image attachments).
  • Device Information: We collect device-specific information, including your device’s Bluetooth identifier, model, operating system, and unique device identifiers to enable Bluetooth connectivity and App functionality.
  • Usage Data: We collect information about how you use the App, such as chat interactions, voice message activity, image attachments, and frequency of use.
  • Content Data: Any voice messages, images, or text you send or receive through the App may be temporarily stored to facilitate communication.
  • Location Data: If you enable location sharing for specific features, we may collect your device’s approximate location to enhance social interactions or group chats. Location data is only collected with your explicit consent.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information to:

  • Enable Bluetooth-based communication, including chat, voice messages, and image attachments.
  • Provide, maintain, and improve the App’s functionality and user experience.
  • Facilitate social media-like interactions, such as connecting with other users or joining group chats.
  • Analyze usage patterns to enhance App performance and develop new features.
  • Ensure the security of the App and prevent unauthorized access or misuse.
  • Comply with legal obligations or respond to lawful requests.

3. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell or rent your personal information. We may share your information in the following cases:

  • With Other Users: Information you share in chats, voice messages, or image attachments may be visible to other users in the App based on your settings (e.g., public or private channels).
  • Service Providers: We may share information with third-party service providers who assist with App operations, such as cloud storage or analytics, under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, such as in response to a court order or government request.
  • Business Transfers: If GIzanTech is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

4. Data Security

We implement reasonable security measures to protect your information, including encryption for voice messages and image attachments. However, no method of transmission over Bluetooth or the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Your Choices

  • Account Information: You can update or delete your profile information within the App at any time.
  • Location Sharing: You can disable location sharing in the App settings or your device’s settings.
  • Bluetooth Access: You can manage Bluetooth permissions through your device’s settings to control connectivity.
  • Opt-Out: You may stop using the App at any time. Uninstalling the App will prevent further data collection.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to provide the App’s services or comply with legal obligations. Content data (e.g., voice messages, images) is stored temporarily and deleted after a reasonable period, unless you request otherwise.

7. Children’s Privacy

The App is not intended for use by individuals under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn such data has been collected, we will take steps to delete it.
